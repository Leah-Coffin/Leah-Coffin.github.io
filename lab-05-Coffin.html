##Part 3 Multicollinearity

Multicollinearity happens when there is high correlation between two or more variables selected, meaning that it is more difficult to say which independent variable is having an effect on the dependent variable.  I do not anticipate that the variables Iâ€™ve selected will be affected by multicollinearity because I have selected three variables that measure different things; ethnicity, income and land vacancy.
If multicollinearity exists we would expect to see an increase in standard errors and the slope moving towrds the null.  By removing one of variables you will see larger slope and statistical significance with the remaining variable.
reg.data <- d
